Classical Mechanics

Course Syllabus

UNIT I: Lagrangian Formulation (14 hrs)

  • 1.1 Review of Newtonian Mechanics: Mechanics of a Particle; Mechanics of a System of Particles; Constraints.
  • 1.2 D' Alembert's principle and Lagrange’s equations; velocity-Dependent potentials and the Dissipation Function.
  • 1.3 Application of Lagrange’s equation to: motion of a single particle in Cartesian coordinate system and plane polar coordinate system; bead sliding on a rotating wire.
  • 1.4 Hamilton’s Principle; Calculus of variations.
  • 1.5 Canonical momentum, cyclic coordinates, and Conservation laws.

UNIT II: Small Oscillations (8 hrs)

  • 2.1 Stable equilibrium and unstable equilibrium; motion near stable equilibrium.
  • 2.2 Small oscillations, resonant frequencies, normal modes, and coordinates.

UNIT III: Central Force Problem (9 hrs)

  • 3.1 Reduction of two-body problem to one-body problem.
  • 3.2 Equation of motion for conservative central forces; energy and angular momentum conservation.

UNIT IV: Hamilton-Jacobi Theory (12 hrs)

  • 4.1 Hamilton-Jacobi Equation for Hamilton’s Principal Function.
  • 4.2 Action-Angle variables; harmonic oscillator problem in action-angle variables.
