Answer to Question 1

Correct Option: (A)

Goal: Derive the ODE for a mass \(m\) on an ideal spring of constant \(k\).

  1. Hooke’s law: spring force \(F_s = -kx\) (restoring, opposite displacement).
  2. Newton’s second law: \(m\ddot{x} = \sum F = -kx\).
  3. Rearrange: \(m\ddot{x} + kx = 0\).

Result: \( \boxed{m\ddot{x} + kx = 0} \).

Answer to Question 2

Correct Option: (D) — Both (A) and (C)

Goal: Solve \( \ddot{x} + \omega^2 x = 0 \).

  1. Characteristic equation: \(r^2 + \omega^2 = 0 \Rightarrow r = \pm i\omega\).
  2. General solution (real form): \(x(t) = A\cos(\omega t) + B\sin(\omega t)\).
  3. Phase form (equivalent): \(x(t) = C\cos(\omega t + \phi)\).

Result: \( \boxed{x(t) = A\cos(\omega t)+B\sin(\omega t) \equiv C\cos(\omega t+\phi)} \).

Answer to Question 4

Correct Option: (C)

Goal: Show total mechanical energy is conserved in ideal SHM.

  1. Kinetic energy: \( T = \tfrac{1}{2}m\dot{x}^2 \).
  2. Potential (spring): \( V = \tfrac{1}{2}kx^2 \).
  3. Total: \( E = T + V = \tfrac{1}{2}m\dot{x}^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}kx^2 \) (constant since no nonconservative forces).

Result: \( \boxed{E = \text{constant}} \).

Answer to Question 7

Correct Option: (B)

  1. Exact equation (about the lowest point): \( \ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{\ell}\sin\theta = 0 \).
  2. Small-angle approximation: \(\sin\theta \approx \theta\).
  3. Linearized ODE: \( \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{\ell}\theta = 0} \).

Answer to Question 17

Correct Option: (C)

For \( m\ddot{x}+c\dot{x}+kx=0 \), write \( \ddot{x}+2\beta\dot{x}+\omega_0^2 x=0 \) with \( \beta=\tfrac{c}{2m} \), \( \omega_0=\sqrt{k/m} \).

  • Underdamped: \( \beta<\omega_0 \) → oscillatory: \( x=A e^{-\beta t}\cos(\omega_d t+\phi) \), \( \omega_d = \sqrt{\omega_0^2-\beta^2} \).
  • Critically damped: \( \beta=\omega_0 \) → fastest return without oscillation.
  • Overdamped: \( \beta>\omega_0 \) → non-oscillatory, slow return.

“Small damping” \(\Rightarrow \beta<\omega_0\) → underdamped oscillation.

Answer to Question 18

Correct Option: (B)

Forced oscillator \( m\ddot{x}+c\dot{x}+kx = F_0\cos(\Omega t) \): steady-state amplitude \[ A(\Omega) = \frac{F_0/m}{\sqrt{(\omega_0^2-\Omega^2)^2 + (2\beta \Omega)^2}} \] is maximized near resonance \( \Omega \approx \omega_0 \) (exact peak slightly below \( \omega_0 \) if \(c\neq 0\)).

Answer to Question 19

Correct Option: (B)

The power dissipated by viscous damping is \(P = F_d \dot{x} = (-c\dot{x})\dot{x} = -c\dot{x}^2 \le 0\) (removes energy). Thus the term \(c\dot{x}\) causes energy loss.

Answer to Question 20

Correct Option: (C)

For \( \ddot{x}+2\beta\dot{x}+\omega_0^2 x=0 \), the motion is oscillatory iff roots are complex: \( \beta < \omega_0 \Rightarrow \omega_d=\sqrt{\omega_0^2-\beta^2} \in \mathbb{R} \). Hence underdamped, oscillatory solution occurs when \( \boxed{\beta<\omega_0} \).
